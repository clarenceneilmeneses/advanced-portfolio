## House style — how my case studies should read

Follow this when writing `project_blocks` content:

- **Voice:** first person, confident but not boastful ("I built…", "I designed…",
  "I shipped…"). Plain, direct sentences. No buzzword soup.
- **Length:** aim for **4–7 blocks** per project. Enough to tell the story,
  not a wall of text. Each `text` block is ~2–4 sentences.
- **Structure (default arc):**
  1. **Opening `text`** — the problem and why it mattered (no heading needed).
  2. `heading` + `text` — what I built / my approach.
  3. One or two `image` blocks with real captions describing what's shown.
  4. `heading` + `text` for a notable technical challenge I solved.
  5. **Closing `text`** — the outcome / what I learned or delivered.
- **Always end with a results or takeaway paragraph** (impact, what I learned,
  or that it shipped/was used in production). Use real outcomes only — if I
  haven't given a number, write the qualitative result, don't invent a metric.
- **Captions** should describe the screenshot specifically ("The admin dashboard
  showing real-time inventory counts"), not generic ("A screenshot").
- **Headings** are short labels (2–5 words): "The problem", "What I built",
  "The challenge", "Results".
- Lead with **impact and my role**, then the tech. Name specific tech only where
  it's relevant to the point being made.
